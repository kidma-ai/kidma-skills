### Mission

Kidma's mission is to **teach artificial intelligence through artificial intelligence**, reimagining education and preparing students for 2030 and beyond. The company brings AI literacy to students in Israeli schools by sending trained instructors into classrooms and providing a proprietary digital platform — the **Kidma Portal** — where students practice hands-on creation with AI tools in a safe, pedagogically supervised environment.

- Sends trained AI instructors into schools for frontal teaching

## 2. The Kidma Portal — Product

The Kidma Portal is a learning management system that enables students to create original outputs based on teacher-defined assignments. It integrates advanced AI models (OpenAI/ChatGPT, DALL·E, Google Gemini, Claude) with full pedagogical mediation by the platform itself.

### Three Pillars

1. **Instructor** — Professional instructor in the classroom (Kidma 1.0 model)
2. **Platform** — AI-powered technology platform
3. **Pedagogy** — Proprietary, research-based pedagogy developed and approved by MoE

### Pain Points the Product Solves

- **Instructors can't help everyone** — A single instructor physically cannot help 30 students refine their prompts simultaneously
- **Multiple tools, knowledge lost** — Students scattered across different AI tools with no continuity
- **Powerful tools, no boundaries** — Students using AI tools without guardrails or educational framing
- **Non-objective evaluations** — Subjective grading without data-backed assessment
- **Gut-feel decisions without data** — Educators making decisions without analytics

### Core AI Agents

- **Real-time prompt feedback agent** — Gives students real-time feedback on their prompts based on the lesson's rules, solving the problem of one instructor being unable to help 30 students simultaneously
- **Assessment agent** — Reviews all of a student's prompts throughout their journey and provides objective, detailed feedback — not just a grade "to hang on the fridge" but actionable takeaways

Every feature was born from actual classroom needs — students, instructors, and school requirements.

### Student Experience

- Students enter the platform after an in-class lesson and navigate to the assigned AI tool (text generation, image creation, video)
- Before a prompt is sent to the AI model, students can "check" their prompt and receive real-time feedback on how to improve it
- The system wraps student prompts to ensure outputs are age-appropriate and aligned with lesson goals
- Sensitive outputs (images, videos) can be routed through instructor approval before reaching the student
- Students submit their work through a built-in submission system

### Teacher/Instructor Experience

- Teachers create lessons in advance with defined objectives, scheduled dates, and available AI tools
- Real-time dashboard showing: who's in the lesson, which tools each student is using, progress status, created outputs, and outputs awaiting approval
- Assessment based on the evolution of students' prompts over time — not just the final product
- Textual and comprehensive assessment (not just numerical)
- Reports on attendance, tool usage, and class progress over time
- Full control to shut down the system or block specific tools dynamically

### Key Differentiators

- **Prompt checking before submission** — teaches meta-skills around communication and precision
- **Prompt wrapping** — pedagogical layer ensuring age-appropriate, task-aligned outputs
- **Human-in-the-loop** — sensitive outputs require instructor approval
- **Process over product assessment** — prompt evolution tracking
- **Full teacher control** — dynamic enable/disable of tools

### AI Tools Integrated

- ChatGPT / OpenAI — text generation, content planning
- DALL·E — image generation
- Midjourney — image generation
- Google Gemini — text and strategy
- Claude (Anthropic) — text generation
- Nano Banana (via Gemini) — image generation
- VEO (via Gemini) — video generation
- Suno — AI music generation
- Hedra — AI avatar / video generation
- D-ID — AI avatar / talking-head video
- Canva — graphic design and presentations
- NotebookLM — self-directed learning, source analysis
- BASE44 — AI-powered no-code website builder
- Perplexity AI — advanced research tool
- Google Translate — language support

> Kidma deliberately uses leading, world-class AI tools — **not** stripped-down "educational" versions — so students learn on the real tools professionals use.

| 6 | **AI Music** | AI-powered music generation. Students explore how AI can compose, mix, and produce musical content and create their own AI-generated music. |
| 7 | **Creating Games with AI** | Students use AI tools to design and build simple games, combining creative design with code generation capabilities. |
| 8 | **Financial Management** | Financial education through AI. Students learn income generation, needs vs wants, compound interest, assets vs liabilities. They create a personal financial calculator web app using AI code generation — first experience generating interactive HTML outputs. |
| 9 | **Completing Products** | Catch-up and refinement session. Students complete, polish, and submit outstanding outputs from previous lessons. |
| 10 | **Text to Speech** | AI-powered text-to-speech technology. Students learn how AI converts written text into natural-sounding voice and create their own audio outputs. |
| 11 | **Final Project** | Comprehensive capstone project integrating all skills and tools learned throughout the course. |

## 4. Teaching Staff Program — "Or" (אור) Program

Kidma's "Or" (Light) program is an AI professional development course for teaching staff. It's built on the belief that AI is not a replacement for teachers but a powerful tool that frees them from technical tasks to focus on human connection, curiosity, and complex thinking. It targets all educators regardless of technological level or subject area.

The program is a **30-hour, gmul-recognized** course registered with the Ministry of Education (**program 47746**, delivered online). Its foundation — and the foundation of Kidma's principal/management education — is the training triangle of **technology + pedagogy + psychology**: mastering the tools, grounding them in sound pedagogy, and understanding the human/psychological dynamics of a classroom (and a staff room) adopting AI.

### 4.1 Version 1 (תשפ"ה) — 10 Sessions

| # | Title | Description |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 1 | **Introduction to AI & the Learning Organization** | Foundational AI concepts, what AI means for educational institutions, first hands-on use. Includes a self-assessment questionnaire. |
| 2 | **The Teacher as Teaching Manager** | Reframing the teacher's role from content deliverer to learning orchestrator who manages AI-augmented instruction. |
| 3 | **Text to Image** | AI image generation for creating educational materials, worksheets, visual aids, and classroom resources. |
| 4 | **Framework Prompts** | Advanced structured prompt engineering using pedagogical frameworks and templates. |
| 5 | **From Product to Learning Process** | Shifting assessment from final outputs to evaluating the learning process itself, using AI to track student prompt evolution. |
| 6 | **Presentations** | Creating professional educational presentations using AI tools (content generation + Canva). |
| 7 | **Personalization in Education** | Using AI to differentiate instruction, create personalized learning paths, and adapt materials to individual student needs. |
| 8 | **Data Analysis** | Leveraging AI to analyze educational data, student performance metrics, and generate actionable insights. |
| 9 | **Asynchronous Session** | Self-paced independent work where teachers implement learned AI skills and document results. |
| 10 | **Summary & Continuation** | Recap, reflection on implementation, planning for continued AI integration beyond the program. |

## 5. Scaling Strategy — Kidma 1.0 → Kidma 2.0

### Kidma 1.0 (Current — Instructor-Led)

- Instructor-led model with Kidma staff in classrooms
- Proven, validated, strong retention

### Kidma 2.0 (Transition Underway — Self-Service Platform)

- **Without Kidma instructor on-site** — platform + pedagogy delivered without physical Kidma staff
- Enables true scaling without operational dependency
- **Already validated:** a growing share of lessons already run without a Kidma instructor on-site

### Strategic Shift

Moving from OpEx (instructor costs per engagement) to CapEx (platform investment). Lower price point dramatically reduces barrier to entry and removes the human bottleneck for growth.

## 7. 2028 Vision — 360° Education Platform

### Core Concept: Flipping the Learning Ratio

- **From:** One teacher for 30 students
- **To:** 30 pedagogical experts for every single student

Imagine every student surrounded by their own team of specialists — a learning companion, a practice coach, a motivation advisor, an assessment expert — all working together to guide that student, in real time, every day.

### Four Components

1. **Student Learning Platform** — Task-based personalized content, dynamic assignments, gamified progress tracking
2. **Teacher Control Panel** — Real-time insights, alerts, and tools to target help where it's needed most
3. **Multi-Agent System** — A network of pedagogical AI agents collaborating to deliver personalized learning journeys
4. **Cognitive Learning Field** — Long-term memory that maintains continuity across grades and schools

### Subject Expansion

- Started with AI as the subject ("teaching AI using AI")
- Principals are already asking: "How do we do this for History, Bible, and Civics?"
- Vision is to expand to all subjects
- Core belief: A multi-agent system that creates personalized learning in every domain is the future of education
